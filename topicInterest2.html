components needed for a basic drumset to function:

Snare drums are usually played with drum sticks or other percussion mallets that can be used to achieve different tones. The snare drum is a versatile percussion instrument due to its sensitivity and responsiveness.

the kick drum (Bass Drum) is used to mark or keep time and makes a low, boom sound when the foot mallet hits or connects with the drumhead.

Toms can be incorporated into a drumset and are typically used the most in constructing drum rolls or fills.

a double-headed tom-tom drum that stands on the floor using three legs and tend to produce a booming, resonant sound which can vary in different pitches.

The throne (Drum Seat) is incredibly important for any prospective drummer. There's no right or wrong choice for a drum seat. Round versus saddle style, soft versus firm, and spindle versus hydraulic are just a few of the myriad options.

The kick drum is struck with a beater attached to a pedal, usually seen on drum kits. This is called the "Kick Drum Pedal" and there are many concepts and designs for pedals as well.

percussion mallets (drum sticks) are used particularly for playing the snare drum, drum kit, and some other percussion instruments. You use these to hit/play your drums.
