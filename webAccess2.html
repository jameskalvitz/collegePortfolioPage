Good VS Bad Accessible Websites

Examples of Accessible and Inaccessible Websites

Good Accessible Website

www.Lonelyplanet.com

This website has an ADHD Friendly Profile and helps those specific visitors focus on specific sections of the website while eliminating distractions. This profile was specially designed to help people with ADHD and Neurodevelopmental disorders browse, read, and focus on the website easily, and is an excellent way to keep users focused on achieving conversion goals.

Bad Accessible Website

www.Airtable.com

The gray color used for the font and border of the login area of the airtable.com user interface fails the contrast ratio guidelines. It affects users with vision impairments (low vision, color blindness, etc.), and even users with good vision in brightly lit situations or low contrast displays.
